## Concept Explanation

Inbound logistics handles product movement from supplier to distribution center. Warehouse operations include receiving, put-away, storage, picking, packing, and dispatch.

## Business Use Case

A truck arrives at the DC with mixed grocery pallets. The receiving team scans ASN (Advanced Shipping Notice) data, checks quantity and quality, assigns storage zones by temperature class, and prepares outbound picks for store orders.

## Users Involved

- Inbound Coordinator: schedules dock appointments
- Receiving Associate: validates incoming shipments
- Inventory Controller: handles stock adjustments
- Warehouse Picker/Packer: prepares outbound store shipments
- QA and Food Safety Teams: verify handling standards

## Tools and Technologies Used

- WMS for task orchestration and inventory tracking
- Barcode/RFID scanning systems
- Temperature and compliance monitoring tools
- TMS for inbound and outbound shipment visibility
