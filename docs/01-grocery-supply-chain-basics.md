## Concept Explanation

The grocery supply chain is the full journey of products from supplier to customer. It includes planning, buying, moving, storing, selling, and replenishing goods. Grocery is different from many industries because products can be perishable, demand changes fast, and stockouts directly impact customer trust.

## Business Use Case

A grocery retailer plans weekly demand for milk, eggs, and produce. Purchase orders are sent to suppliers. Products arrive at a distribution center, move to stores, and are sold to customers. If demand spikes during a holiday week, replenishment must react quickly to avoid empty shelves.

## Users Involved

- Supply Chain Planner: predicts demand and creates plans
- Buyer: places and manages purchase orders
- Distribution Center (DC) Team: receives, stores, and dispatches stock
- Store Manager: monitors shelf availability and local demand
- Transportation Coordinator: manages shipment schedules

## Tools and Technologies Used

- ERP systems for planning and procurement
- Warehouse Management System (WMS) for stock movement in DCs
- Transportation Management System (TMS) for shipment planning
- Point of Sale (POS) for sales signal capture
- Reporting dashboards for inventory and fill-rate tracking
